Content Security (DRM) with the Native Player SDKs

Digital rights management (DRM) protects your video content by encrypting the video data and unlocking it based on license policies.

The Brightcove Native Player SDK platforms support the following DRM solutions:

Platform DRM Solution
Native Player SDK for iOS Apple's FairPlay Streaming
Native Player SDK for tvOS Apple's FairPlay Streaming
Native Player SDK for Android Google's Widevine Modular

Note: the use of Widevine Classic has been deprecated. We encourage you to migrate to FairPlay Streaming.

Video Cloud customers

Follow these steps to create DRM-enabled content:

  1. Contact your account manager to have your account(s) DRM-enabled.

    Note: to use DRM encryption, your account must be enabled for these features. Contact your account manager for more information.

  2. Create custom ingest profiles with defined packages for each DRM type (Widevine Modular or FairPlay).

  3. In Studio, in the Upload module, you should now see your custom DRM profiles in the Ingest Profile dropdown menu. Here is an example of what your menu might look like:

    Note: you can also use Dynamic Ingest to create DRM-enabled content based on your ingest profiles.

  4. Upload your content using one of your ingest profiles.

  5. For existing content in Video Cloud, you can use the Retranscode button in the Media module and select your desired ingest profile. Retranscoding works only if you initially used the new Studio's Upload module or Dynamic Ingest.

Perform customers

Your video content will need to be one of the following formats:

  1. For Widevine Modular, you will need to produce DRM-protected content which uses MPEG-DASH manifests with segmented and encrypted videos.

  2. For FairPlay, you will need to produce DRM-protected content which uses HLS manifests with segmented and encrypted videos.

Now that you know how to create DRM renditions, you are ready to learn how to work with your media content.
